HR Analytics Dashboard Insights:

1. Overall Summary:
   - Total Employees: 1,470
   - Total Attrition: 237 employees
   - Attrition Rate: 16.1%
   - Average Age: 37 years
   - Average Salary: 6.5K
   - Average Tenure: 7 years

2. Attrition by Gender:
   - Higher attrition among Males (140) compared to Females (79).

3. Attrition by Education:
   - Life Sciences (38%) and Medical (27%) show the highest attrition.
   - Marketing (15%) and Technical Degree (14%) are moderate.
   - Other education fields account for 5%.

4. Attrition by Age Group:
   - Highest attrition in the 26–35 age group (116).
   - Younger group 18–25 (44) and 36–45 (43) also show notable attrition.
   - Attrition drops sharply after 45+, with lowest in 55+ (8).

5. Attrition by Salary Slab:
   - Majority of attrition occurs in the lowest salary band (Up to 5K) with 163 employees.
   - Attrition decreases as salary increases:
     - 5K–10K: 49
     - 10K–15K: 20
     - 15K+: 5

6. Attrition by Years at Company:
   - Peak attrition around the 1-year mark.
   - Steep drop after early years, with relatively lower attrition among long-tenured employees.
   - A smaller spike is visible around 8–9 years.

7. Attrition by Job Role:
   - Laboratory Technician (62) and Sales Executive (57) have the highest attrition counts.
   - Research Scientist (47) and Sales Representative (33) also show significant attrition.

8. Key Takeaways:
   - Attrition is concentrated among:
     - Mid-age employees (26–35),
     - Lower salary slabs,
     - Early tenure (especially around 1 year),
     - Specific roles like Laboratory Technician and Sales Executive.
   - Focusing retention strategies on compensation, early engagement, and role-specific interventions can reduce overall attrition and improve workforce stability.
